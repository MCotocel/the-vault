  • A market is a collection of buyers and sellers in an area
  • Types of markets include:
    • Consumer goods market:
      • Products such as food, cosmetics, and furniture
    • Service market:
      • Services such as cleaning and banking
    • Housing market:
      • Buying and selling properties
    • Commodity market:
      • Raw materials such as oil, copper, and wheat
  • Marketing refers to a company undertaking activities to promote a product or a service
    • This involves:
      • Identifying the needs and wants of the customers
      • Designing products that meet those needs
      • Understanding threats from competitors
      • Telling customers about products
      • Charging the right price
      • Persuading customers to buy the products
      • Making products available in convenient locations
  • To build customer relationships, a business must:
    • Personalise communication
    • Know the customer
    • Connect regularly
    • Build trust
    • Take complaints very seriously
  • To keep customer loyalty, a business must:
    • Give away reward cards and free gifts
    • Give away donations to charity
    • Have partnership deals
  • There are two types of orientations:
    • Production orientation
      • This is where the business develops products based on what it's good at doing
    • Marketing orientation
      • This is where the business responds to customer needs and wants
  • Market share is the percentage of an industry that is earned by a company
    • It is calculated with the following formula:
    • $Market\ share = \frac{Total\ product\ or\ business\ sales}{Total\ sales\ in\ the\ whole\ market} \times 100$
  • Niche marketing is marketing concentrated on a small but specific segment of the population
  • Mass marketing is marketing that appeals to the whole market, ignoring specific segments
