  • The product life cycle is the growth and decline of a product
    • First comes the introduction
      • This is when the product is launched
      • Sales grow slowly since people aren't aware of the product
      • Advertising is heavily used
      • There is usually low to no profit
    • Next is growth
      • This is where sales start to grow rapidly
      • Persuasive advertising is used
      • The price of the product may be decreased
      • Profits start to rise
    • Maturity comes after
      • Sales increase at a slower rate
      • There is a lot of competition
      • Competitive pricing tends to be used
      • Advertising expenditure is at its highest
      • Profits will soon start to fall
    • Decline comes last
      • Sales will fall
      • The product loses its appeal
      • Advertising is reduced
      • Production may be stopped
  • Extensions strategies are strategies to extend the range of a product
    • These include:
      • Finding new markets
      • Finding new uses for the product
      • Modifying the product
      • Developing the product range
      • Changing the appearance or packaging
      • Encouraging more frequent use of the product
  • The Boston Matrix is a model to help businesses analyse portfolios of businesses
    • There are four categories
      • Stars are high-growth products in a market where they are stronger than the competition
      • Cash cows are low-growth products with a high market share
        • They are mature products which have already been successful
      • Question marks are products with low market share in high-growth markets
        • This suggests that they have potential but they need investment
      • Dogs refers to products with a low market share in low-growth products
