  • A vertical or tall organisational structure is a hierarchical structure with many levels of responsibility
    • These are usually used in large organisations
    • Advantages of these structures include:
      • The lines of communication are clearer
      • There are plenty of opportunities for promotions
    • Disadvantages of these structures include:
      • Slow decision-making
      • Distorted messages
      • High management costs
  • A horizontal or flat organisational structure is a hierarchical structure with only a few levels of responsibility
    • These are usually used in small organisations
    • Advantages of these structures include:
      • Communication from top to bottom will be easier
      • Fewer mistakes in communication will occur
    • Disadvantages of these structures include:
      • Blurred job roles
      • Loss of control
  • Delegation is when tasks are handed down to employees
    • The manager still has responsibility for the task, but the subordinate completes the task
  • Centralisation is decision making that is only the organisation's key decisions
  • Decentralisation is decision making that is affected by subordinates
  • A matrix structure can also be used
    • A matrix structure is where employees are separated into teams
