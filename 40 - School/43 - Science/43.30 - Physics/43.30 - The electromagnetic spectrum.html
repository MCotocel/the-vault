  • The electromagnetic spectrum is a spectrum of waves
    • The further on the left they are, the lower frequency, the lower energy, and the larger wavelength they have
    • All waves travel at the same speed, 300,000,000 m/s
      • This is the speed of light in a vacuum

  • Radio waves have the longest wavelengths
    • They are mainly used for communication
    • They are emitted by a transmitter
    • When they arrive, the information they carry is received
  • Microwaves are used for communication, radar, and cooking food
    • Rader used the waves to find the position of things
    • Microwaves cook food quickly because the water molecules in food absorb the microwaves and become hot
      • For this reason, microwaves have metal screens to reflect microwaves so that they do not heat people up
  • Infrared is given off by all objects
    • The hotter an object is, the more energy is emitted
    • Cameras have been made specifically to detect infrared waves
    • Infrared waves are also used in remote controls since they are not harmful
  • Visible light is the light that is visible to the human eye
    • We use visible light to see
    • It is also sent along optical fibres to communicate
  • Ultraviolet light is emitted by the sun
    • It is harmful to the eyes and can damage the skin
    • This is why you get a tan when exposed to the sun for a long time
    • The ozone layer absorbs a lot of UV radiation
    • Some chemicals glow when under UV light
  • X-rays pass through soft body tissue, but they cannot pass through bones
    • This allows them to be used to check someone's bones
    • Sustained exposure to x-rays can cause cancer
      • For this reason, radiographers are at risk, so they must stand behind lead shields when taking x-rays
  • Gamma rays are like x-rays, in the fact that they can penetrate objects
    • They damage living cells, and can cause mutations which lead to cancer
    • They are generally used to sterilise medical instruments, kill microorganisms, and to treat cancer
