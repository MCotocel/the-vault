  • When a ray of light hits a flat mirror, it is reflected so that the angle of incidence is equal to the angle of reflection
    • The angle of incidence is the angle at which the incidence ray hits the surface
    • The angle of reflection is the angle at which the reflected ray bounces off
    • The normal is the point the ray hits
  • On a rough surface, the angle of incidence is not equal to the angle of reflection
  • Refraction is when the speed of the wave changes
    • When the wave goes through a more dense material, it slows down
      • When this happens, the wave bends towards the normal
    • It then returns to the normal speed when exiting and returning to the material from before
      • At this point, it bends away from the normal
  • Different materials can bend rays of light by different amounts
    • This is measured using the refractive index
    • The larger it is, the more light will be refracted
  • Different materials have different critical angles
    • When the angle of incidence is larger than the critical angle, total internal reflection occurs
    • This is where the wave is completely reflected
    • When the angle of incidence is smaller than the critical angle, refraction occurs
