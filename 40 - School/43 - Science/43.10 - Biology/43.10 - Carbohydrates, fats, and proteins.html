Carbohydrates, fats, and proteins

  • Carbohydrates → Glucose
    • Carbohydrates are made up of carbon, hydrogen, and oxygen
    • There are different types of carbohydrates:
      • Monosaccharides: Simple sugars (glucose)
      • Disaccharides: Double sugars (sucrose)
      • Polysaccharides: Large molecules (starch)
  • Fat → Fatty acids and glycerol
    • Fats are made up of carbon, hydrogen, nitrogen, and sulphur
    • Fats are also known as lipids
    • Most lipids are triglycerides (true fats/neutral fats)
    • Other lipids include waxes, steroids, and cholesterol
  • Proteins → Amino acids
    • Proteins are made up of carbon, hydrogen, nitrogen, and sulphur
    • Proteins are a group of large, complex polymer molecules, made up of long chains of amino acids
    • Proteins have a variety of roles:
      • Structurer: Component of body tissues
      • Catalytic: Enzymes are proteins
      • Signaling: Hormones and receptors
      • Immunological: Antibodies are proteins
    • The reactants are polymers, long molecules
    • The products are monomers, singe molecules
