Hormones

  • Hormones are secretes by endocrine glands into blood vessels that pass through the glands
  • The endocrine system:
    • Works by hormones that are transmitted through the bloodstream
    • Takes longer to react and is slow
    • Has a longer-lasting response than the nervous system
    • Can have widespread effects on several different organs
  • There are several different glands in the body:
Gland Hormone Functions
Pituitary Follicle stimulation hormone, luteinising hormone, antidiuretic hormone Stimulates egg development and oestrogen in females, and sperm and testosterone production in males
Thyroid Thyroxine Controls metabolic rate
Pancreas Insulin, glucagon Lowers and raises blood glucose
Adrenals Adrenaline Prepares the body for physical activity
Testes (Males) Testosterone Controls the development of secondary male sexual characteristics
Ovaries (Females) Oestrogen, progesterone Controls the development of secondary female sexual characteristics, regulates the menstrual cycle
  • The pancreas is an endocrine and an exocrine gland
    • It secretes hormones regulating blood glucose in addition to secreting enzymes into the small intestines
  • Adrenaline is a hormone secreted by the adrenal glands
    • It increases the breathing rate to take more oxygen into the body
    • The heart beats faster, which sends more blood to the muscles, causing them to receive more glucose and oxygen for respiration
    • Blood is diverted from the intestine to the muscles
    • Glycogen, stored carbohydrate, is converted into glucose and released into the blood by the liver
    • The pupils dilate, which increases visual sensitivity
    • Body hair stands upright, causing the animal to appear larger
    • Mental awareness is increased, decreasing reaction times
