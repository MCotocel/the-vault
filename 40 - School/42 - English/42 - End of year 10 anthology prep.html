In 'Beyond the Sky and Earth', how does Zeppa present her experience in Bhutan?

Zeppa presents her experience in Bhutan in 'Beyond the Sky and Earth' by using a variety of vocabulary choices. For example, at the beginning, she writes: "I watch mountains rise to meet the moon". This statement implies that she is in awe of the mountains, since she is watching them, observing them reaching for the moon, a faraway object. Additionally, since the mountains are rising to "meet the moon", she may feel claustrophobic and suffocated by them, due to them being huge in comparison to the surroundings.

Later on in the extract, she says "I stay close to them, hoping to pick up some of their enthusiasm". Her hoping to become more enthusiastic about Bhutan shows that she isn't already enthusiastic, implying that she's underwhelmed by the town and doesn't like what she has seen so far. This tells us that her experience has not been great up to this point, and she may possibly regret going to Bhutan.

At the end, Zeppa states that she is "full of admiration for this small country". This clearly shows that her opinions on Bhutan have changed, and that she appreciates and enjoys it. This is shown through usage of the word 'admiration', which implies that she has been impressed by the small town.

In 'H is for Hawk' how does the writer use language and structure to interest and engage the reader?

The writer uses foreshadowing to interest the reader in 'H is for Hawk'. For example, the quote "Don't want you going home with the wrong bird" hints to the reader that exactly that may happen. The fact that it is a line of dialogue that is seemingly out of place causes most readers to overlook it, thinking it unimportant. However, it does foreshadow the ending of the extract, where she almost does go home with the wrong bird.

Furthermore, suspense and tension is also used in the extract. An example of this is shown in the quote "Scratching talons, another thump. And another. Thump". The use of short, brief sentences builds tension throughout the paragraph, engaging the reader and making them curious as to what happens next. Additionally, the use of onomatopoeia in 'thump' shows that something is moving, along with implying that the hawk is aggressive due to its hostile sounds.

The author further engages the reader by adding a turning point. This occurs when the quote "It was the wrong bird" is said. The quote inserts a twist into the extract, surprising the reader and making them eager to read on in order to see what happens. Additionally, a short while later the interjection of "Oh." shows that the writer was disappointed, interesting the reader as to why that is, yet again convincing them to read more of the extract.

How does the writer use language and structure in 'Explorers or boys messing about?' to show what people thought about the actions of the two explorers?

The writer uses cartoonish imagery to show what is thought of the two explorers. This is shown when the verbs 'plunged' and 'plucked' are used. These show that the explorers were treated like helpless children, since not only are cartoons mainly for children, they are also portrayed as unable to save themselves from their crash and needed to be 'plucked' from the sea.

Furthermore, the two men are heavily mocked in the extract. An example of this is shown when it is said that they were "boys messing around with a helicopter". Not only does this portray them in an infantile manner, as if unable to do anything, it also mocks them and implies that they were clueless and did not know what they were doing when flying the helicopter.

The writer further discredits the explorers through the line "It sounds as if they were pushing it [the helicopter] to the maximum". This discredits the two men by showing that they were not prepared to fly a helicopter out into the sea, and took unnecessary risks when doing it as well. Additionally, they are further discredited in the quote "The flying conditions had been 'excellent'". This implies that even if it should have been simple to fly a helicopter under these circumstances, the explorers managed to fail even then, which discredits them.
