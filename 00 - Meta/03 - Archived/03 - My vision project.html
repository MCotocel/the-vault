[Slide 1] I'd just like to preface this by letting you all know that I wrote this at 1 AM while half-asleep, so don't judge it too harshly.

[Slide 2] Anyway, I was assigned the chapter "Banks of the creek", and it mainly focuses of the industries the UAE has been in.

[Slide 3] So, the economy of the UAE has long depended on the pearl industry. These pearls were sought after by divers, taking risks in order to find one of the precious gems of the sea. However, pearl diving wasn't the only industry that the UAE delved into. The UAE was an important transit point for trading around the world. Ships would come to the ports to leave and take cargo all around the world.

[Slide 4] However, during the Great Depression of the 1930s, the demand for natural pearls collapsed. Additionally, Japan's cultured pearls, which were much cheaper than real pearls, played a part in the collapse of the pearl industry.

Due to this, the UAE shifted to transporting cargo and fishing. This was further benefited by the fact that early on, the UAE had a tax-free economy.

[Slide 5] In 1937, the UAE signed an agreement for sole proprietorship of oil exploration in the country. It took 20 months to negotiate, and granted the UAE rights to exploration for a total of 75 years, with an annual royalty of 30k Indian rupees. However, oil was not found, and the UAE put the project on hold.

Oh, and by the way, at one point, gold was implied to be smuggled into and out of the Indian subcontinent by the news, but it was actually legally imported and exported.

[Slide 6] At some point, they built something called 'The creek' was a 14lkm long harbour, made for small and medium-sized dhows. Ships loaded with up to 800 tonnes of goods could use the creek's facilities. In 1987, the number of berths was increased to 35, allowing more ships to use the harbour.

[Slide 7] In 1966, oil was discovered at the Fateh field. The first oil shipment left on the 22nd of September, 1969. This marked the beginning of the UAE's success in the industry of oil.

[Slide 8] I found this chapter pretty interesting, since it went into detail about what the UAE did before they discovered oil. However, at times it kinda went off on a tangent, like that bit about gold I mentioned earlier. Still, overall, pretty good chapter.
