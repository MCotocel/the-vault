notes

Organising knowledge with multi-level content is a paper by Francis Miller about note-taking and 01 - Personal knowledge management. I've summarised the content below:

  • Intro
    • Two main problems
    • Structure
    • All knowledge has structure
    • Sense of knowledge means an understanding of the structure
    • No visual representation means more cognitive energy is required
    • Multiple knowledge levels
    • Knowledge has hierarchical structure
    • Top level is the summary
    • Bottom level is the detail
    • Problems arise when levels are not differentiated
    • Different people have different needs for detail
    • Missing knowledge levels means more friction
    • Solutions
    • Structure
    • Knowledge maps
    • Multiple knowledge levels
    • Explicit differentiation
  • Structure
    • All knowledge has structure
    • Many different ways to structure
    • Difficult to find structure in linear content
    • Implicit structure
    • No explicit structure
    • Linear text
    • Explicit structure
    • Spoken words
    • Text
    • Visuals
  • Knowledge maps
    • Explicit
      • Visual
      • Shows relationships
      • Examples:
        • Big:
          • Cognitive bias codex
          • History timeline map
          • Megatrends and technologies
        • Small:
          • Topic maps
          • Book summary
        • Big:
          • Lots of information
          • Lots of connections
        • Small:
          • Easy to read
          • Not much detail
      • Benefits:
        • Learners:
          • Lots of knowledge
          • Displays relationships
          • Simplify subject
          • Reduce information overload
          • Accessible
          • More interesting
        • Creators:
          • Identify knowledge gaps
          • Identify patterns
          • New categories
      • Problems:
        • Can contain too much detail
  • Hierarchical knowledge organisation
    • Top level is general information
    • Bottom level is specific information
  • Multi-level content
    • Hierarchy
      • Benefits:
        • Learners:
          • More between big picture and detail
          • Identify critical information easily
          • Scan key ideas
          • Clarity about components of a topic
          • Easier to revise ideas
          • Assess progress made
        • Creators:
          • Helps communicating effectively
          • Helps improve thinking process
        • Uses:
          • Non-fiction books:
            • Problems of readers:
            • Hard to get started
            • Losing interest
            • Lacking big picture
            • Overwhelmed by detail
            • Losing the thread
            • Forgetting book's ideas
            • Struggling on what action to take
      • Applying multi-level content:
        • Book summary with knowledge map
        • Chapter summaries
        • Content summaries
      • Benefits:
        • Summaries provide easy way to get started
        • Summaries show if book is worth reading
        • Summaries help refresh memories
        • Easy to revisit strategies
      • Businesses:
        • Develop custom megatrends map
        • Explain strategies to staff
        • Explain strategies to external groups
      • Education: - Understand relations - Simplify learning - Hide details
      • Moving between levels:
        • Zooming in:
          • Going in detail
          • Subtopics
        • Zooming out:
          • Summarise
          • Big picture
      • Reorganising:
        • Produce new meaning to notes
        • See relationships
      • Combining:
        • Relationships
        • Wider context
  • Problem of structure
    • All knowledge has structure
    • Types:
      • Taxonomy
      • Timeline
      • Story
      • Casual explanation
      • Argument/case
      • Process/sequence
      • Description
      • Content structure
      • Relationships
    • Implicit:
      • Structure is implied through text, but not shown
    • Explicit:
      • Structure is show visually
    • Small vs big knowledge maps:
      • Small:
        • Positives:
          • Easy to produce
        • Negatives:
          • Not much detail
      • Big:
        • Positives:
          • Large amount of information
          • Space for connections
        • Negatives:
          • Time-consuming
          • Overwhelming
    • Benefits of knowledge maps:
      • Learners:
        • Large amount of information
        • Shows connections
        • Simplifies knowledge
        • Less daunting
        • Accessible knowledge
      • Creators:
        • Identify gaps in knowledge
        • Develop new categories
        • Problems:
        • Can get complex quickly
        • Problem of multiple knowledge levels
    • Hierarchical knowledge organization:
      • Different scales
      • Elaboration
      • From big picture to detail
      • From important information to less important
      • From general to specific
      • Wider focus to narrower
    • Going into detail:
      • Creating multi-level content
      • Summarize material
      • Re-organize elements
      • Combine elements
    • Rules:
      • Don't make clusters too large
      • Information in cluster should be organised
      • Overlapping information in clusters allowed
      • Cross-reference information in clusters
