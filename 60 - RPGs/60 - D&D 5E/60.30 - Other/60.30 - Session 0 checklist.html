dnd

Experience

Earning XP

  • Killing monsters awards XP
  • Peaceful resolutions award more XP

Milestones

  • Milestones will also be used to award XP

Level-up

  • Levelling up takes place after a long rest when you have enough XP

Behaviour

Phone usage

  • Minimal phone usage at the table if it's not D&D related (unless it's urgent)
    • Phones can be used for D&D beyond character sheets and any D&D notes
  • Phones can be used during breaks
  • No headphones or earbuds at the table

Player attention

  • Players should be attentive even if they are not currently taking any actions
  • Repeated lack of attention is a problem

Unannounced dice rolls

  • Dice rolls only count when the DM asks for them
  • If they are rolled without the DM asking for them, they do not count

Dice rolls against other PCs

  • Dice rolls against one another must be used sparingly, and only when needed

PvP

  • PvP combat, arguments, and tension is allowed, as long as it is in-character and makes sense

PC secrets

  • PCs are allowed to keep secrets from other PCs and the DM in-game

Player expectations

  • Players can play for different reasons
  • If a player plays for one reason that doesn't have much action (e.g. roleplaying), they may talk to the DM so that a solution may be found

Boundaries

  • Boundaries will be established during session 0
  • A player may step out at any time or call a time-out if they feel uncomfortable
  • A player may take the DM aside during breaks to mention any boundaries they want established

Sexual content

  • No sexual content
  • Romance is allowed, but will result in a fade-to-black

Torture

  • Torture can occur, but will not be detailed

Slavery

  • Both NPCs and PCs can become enslaved by captors

Player agency

  • Players have control over decisions and can alter the game world, within reason

Game balance

  • It is possible to encounter a high level creature early on

Rule debates

  • Players can debate the rules during the game
  • The DM will look the rule up in the moment

Meta-knowledge

  • Players should not abuse meta-knowledge of the game when possible

Min-maxing

  • Optimised characters are tolerated, as long as it doesn't become an issue
  • Unoptimised are welcome as well

Character-party fit

  • Characters should ideally be part of the party
  • Characters can play well with other characters, but this does not mean race and class choice should be restricted

Murder hobos

  • Murdering everyone is permitted, but there will be in-game consequences

Food and drinks

  • Water will always be supplied
  • Food may sometimes be supplied, but not always
    • Putting a little money towards the food is appreciated
  • Bringing snacks or food for everyone awards you with free inspiration

DM

DM style

  • Rules are not a strict guideline, and can be bent slightly
  • There will be homebrew content
  • RAI will usually take priority over RAW

Player absences

  • Player absences should ideally be declared a day before the session, and the session will be rescheduled if possible
  • If not possible, the rest of the party will perform activities that aren't directly related to the story
  • If more that 2 people don't turn up, the party will take a side quest or downtime activities
  • Repeated absences will result in consequences

Player narrative

  • Players have narrative authority
  • Players can freely create backstories with help from the DM for the campaign

Character creation

Statistics

  • Only rolling for stats is permitted
  • You can reroll once for your lowest stat if you wish to

Alignment

  • Alignment must be followed when roleplaying
  • Repeatedly taking actions opposed to your alignment may cause it to change

Flavour

  • Players can work with the DM to flavour their characters, making minor alterations for the sake of roleplaying

Game style and mechanics

Type

  • The game is a semi-sandbox, in which they can go anywhere they wish almost from the start
  • The game will be high fantasy, with some cosmic horror aspects

Campaign length

  • The campaign has no set time, but characters will probably reach level 20 by the end of it

Factions

  • Various factions, both good and evil, exist
  • PCs may join any of these factions, and they don't all need to be in the same one

Start

  • All PCs will start at 1st level
  • They will be new to the town they begin in, but their backstory may influence how certain areas address them

Backgrounds

  • Backgrounds could affect their standing in some towns
  • Backgrounds will be decided in session 0 by the DM working with the players

Downtime

  • PCs may take activites or individual side quests during downtime to develop their background and possibly skills

Resting

  • Short rests will give a 5 minute break to the players
  • Long rests will give a 10-20 minute break to the players
  • Roleplaying is allowed during rests between PCs while other players take breaks
    • You will also have an optional five minutes afterwards to continue roleplaying
  • You may only take three short rests before needing a long rest

Critical hits

Damage

  • Critical hits allow you to automatically get full damage, roll again, and add modifiers

Critical successes

  • A Nat-20 does not automatically mean a success, especially if the roll circumstances are near-impossible

Critical fails

  • A Nat-1 does not automatically mean a terrible fail

Vendors

  • Street vendors can haggle with the players
  • Premium shops will not haggle

Spells in environments

  • Spells can affect environments, such as a lightning spell on water dealing damage to all creatures touching the water

Death

Death

  • A PC can be resurrected if they choose to do so, otherwise a new character can be created
  • Every player should ideally have a backup character

Resurrection

  • PCs can be resurrected even if the other party members do not have a cleric by paying a cleric to do so
