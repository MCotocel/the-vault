Stanley, the sentient room

The players enter a very sparse room. Within the room, there is a coffin and a small box around the size of a fist with a small button on the front. Within the coffin, there is a single dagger. From the box, a voice comes out. "Hello. To proceed, please kill one of your party members and leave them in the coffin. If you have any questions, please feel free to press the button and ask me. My name is Stanley."

Now, unless your party REALLY hates one another, their first instinct shouldn't be to talk to kill a party member. Instead, they'll probably talk to Stanley for a bit.

The solution is simple: tell Stanley "no" or kill a member. If you do either, he creates a door and lets you through. Stanley is literally the room, so he can feel what is in the coffin and knows when the group is trying to cheat. So, have some fun being Stanley. Figure out how he became a room (I told my party that he was created by a wizard and given sentience) and just play around, because Stanley is pretty damn bad at his job.

There are a lot of ways you can have fun with your players. Stanley could get emotional as the party says their last goodbyes and give them a hint. He might give a hint because he's entertained by them or flattered by how much attention they're giving him. Maybe he acts disappointed when he sees them decide to kill someone. Maybe he accidentally says that "some groups kill a member, I mean, all groups. All groups kill a member." Let your players relax a bit talking to Stanley and have him prod the party along with subtle hints, and watch them groan when they discover the simplicity of the answer to the dungeon.
