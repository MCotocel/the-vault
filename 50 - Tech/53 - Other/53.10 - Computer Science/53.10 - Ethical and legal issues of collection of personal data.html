  • There are a few issues associated with the collection of private data:
    • Privacy:
      • Who has access to the data?
      • How is access controlled?
    • Ownership:
      • Who owns the data about you?
      • Right to erasure (right to remove information about you from devices)
    • Consent:
      • How is your data collected?
      • Opt-in as opposed to opt-out
    • Misuse:
      • Data can be acquired by criminals through hacking
    • Data protection:
      • How is your data protected?
      • Your rights
      • Data protection laws (e.g GDPR)
  • The 'Data Protection Act' is the UK's implementation of the GDPR
    • Data collection must be transparent and fair
    • Data must be collected only for legitimate reasons
    • Data must be relevant and limited when possible
    • Data must be accurate and kept up to date
    • Data must not be kept for longer than necessary
    • Data must be processed in a secure manner
    • Data controllers must prove that data protection measures are sufficient
  • Computer Misuse Act 1990:
    • Illegal to have unauthorised access to data with the intent to:
      • Commit further offences
      • Modify data
    • Examples include:
      • Fraud
      • Unauthorised work
      • Planting of viruses
      • Hacking
      • Deliberate data destruction
      • Data theft
      • Fraud
  • Cookies are small plain text files stored on your computer by a browser
    • They are used to keep track of usage of a website, including:
      • Resuming where you left off when you revisit the site
      • Remembering registered login
      • Remembering theme selections
      • Remembering preferences
      • Customising browsing experience
    • Website operators using cookies must:
      • Tell users cookies are being used
      • Explain what the cookies are doing
      • Obtain consent to use cookies
